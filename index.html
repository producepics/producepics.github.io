Frequently Asked Questions

Does one need new (and expensive) equipment?

The strength of the system is its simplicity - it is accessible via a smartphone and does not require any additional equipment. Pictures may be taken on the farm, uploaded later from the pack-house and still be able to trigger a PACA (and blockchain) based smartcontract.

What is PACA?

The Perishable Agricultural Commoditities Act (PACA) was enacted at the request of the produce industry. PACA protects the produce industry by establishing and enforcing a code of fair business practices and helping companies resolve business disputes.

Will the cost of Quality / USDA Inspections be reduced?

By eliminating the need for a certified inspector to travel and by including a robust image processing platform, we anticipate the cost of inspections to go down from around US $ 250 per inspections to around $ 25 per inspection.

Does one require stable WiFi connections at the Farm?

Most field areas in a farm do not receive good WiFi signals. The app allows a grower to take pictures on a smartphone first and then upload later from the office, etc.

Why use Blockchain?

PACA contracts are paper based "smart contracts". By implementing PACA compliant smart contracts in Blockchain one can introduce smart triggers and automatic payments etc. to improve the overall efficiency and reliability.

Will the wait times for Quality / USDA Inspections be reduced?

Travel time and the need to drive to multiple facilities contribute to large wait times, often in the range of a few hours to upto 25 hours. Use of the roubst image processing platform reduces wait times for inspections.

Reducing Food Loss

Roughly, one third of the food produced in the world goes to waste. The founding team has been using imaging (including hyperspectral imaging) technologies to address food loss and wastage. After extensive research with growers, distributors, and retailers, the team realized that the PACA framework combined with latest image processing techniques can help.

Hyperspectral Imaging

Hyperspectral imaging capabilities are expected to be available on smartphones in the next two years.

Such imaging capabilities will provide more accurate analysis of produce quality and shelf-life.

  • Smartphone based
  • No expensive new equipment
  • Superior analysis

At present, hyperspectral cameras cost close to $ 20K. Once they become available on the smartphones, the platform will enable everyone to conduct hyperspectral analysis.

PACA and Blockchain

PACA is an existing smart contract framework in the US Produce industry.

PACA is a natural fit for blockchain technology.

  • Users are already familiar with PACA.
  • PACA facilitates easy transition to blockchain technology.
  • Teachnology based smartcontracts will greatly improve efficiency and productivity.

New technology adoption can take years, however PACA will make it easier for users to adopt blockchain technology.

Shelf Life Models

Accurate shelf-life models are important as produce has no value beyond its shelf-life and must be discarded.

Reliable remaining shelf-life estimates will reduce wastage in produce industry.

  • Non-destructive and frequent analysis.
  • More accurate estimates.
  • Easy and economical to implement.

As ecommerce (and delivery) options emerge for the produce industry, it becomes critical to estimate the remaining shelf life. Customers often buy based on perceived "remaining shelf life".

Global Image Database

Smartphones can auto-detect and auto-encode location (GPS), date/time stamps.

Images combined with GPS and Time stamps provide very valuable metadata about growth season, location, etc.

  • Differences due to growth regions
  • Differences due to growth season
  • Differenes due to growth conditions

Very comprehensive database that improves over time. Will become more reliable as the analysis can incorporate variations in produe due to growth region, season, etc.
